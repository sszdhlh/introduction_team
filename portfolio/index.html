Design Inspiration

Poster-click picture to see the pdf of poster

My intention with this concept is to help certain youngsters who are of playing age and want to play but can't find a local playground or a friend to play with. Therefore, the goal of my design for this app is to assist kids in finding the closest playground and pals they may play with. As a result, you won't have to stress about finding a playground to play at or finding other kids to play with when you want to have fun. The primary target group for this initiative is children between the ages of 0 and 12 who are looking for the closest playground and buddies to visit an amusement park with. My concept was to develop an app that would help users meet friends if they went to the playground and found nobody to play with, as well as provide a route from their home to the closest playground. He can start a team event to reserve a friend for a specific playground so that other friends can participate once they learn about it. And make a personal page to save their preferred playgrounds so they can check the pertinent details at any time, then filter in their preferred list the next time they have time.

Reflection:

I believe that our design is seriously wrong because of the initially limited amount of time for rapid design. For instance, the target audience includes some kids who aren't sure what age they should be, and there are questions about features like whether to log in to use the software, whether the feature is too basic or too similar to Google Maps and how to effectively encourage kids to go outside and play. to do this, our team talked about adjustments that may be made for the target audience. The later website architecture can still be implemented and readily improved because the fundamental design has already been accomplished.

Design Proposal Milestone

Our website's primary purpose during the prototyping phase is to locate playgrounds and create upcoming playground events. You can carry out both tasks without logging in. Developed an event page, a teammate search page, and a map page. The search box was placed below the map in the style of the original map page, and the map has several arrows on the final website. Each of them stands in for a playground, and by clicking on the arrows, the user can view all of the playground's specifics. The event creation feature is on the details page; click the Find Teammate button to access it. At the same time, our team has established an event page. On this page, users can locate playgrounds for various skill levels. By clicking Join Now, they can select one of them. Fill up the booking form and finish the creative task. The add-to-team function on our final website ultimately didn't work. On the Find Teammates page, users can only see all events that have been created.

Work in Progress Milestone

The team group chose to give the development of two essential functions—finding playgrounds and starting team events—a priority when the prototype design was finished. Since I am the only backend engineer on our 45-person team, I wrote all of the logic code for the milestone release by myself. Our project is now capable of showing certain playgrounds on the home page. The map interface shows all playground sites and their details, and the ability to filter results by keyword is provided below. The first demonstration, however, did not include the implementation of the team event launch. We also demonstrate each of the features in turn in the demonstrations that follow.

Reflection:

In the link, while not all of the functions have been implemented, we have done so for one crucial function—locating playgrounds. At the bottom of the page, users can browse playground locations and details as well as keyword filters. But not all of the features have been put into practice yet. The majority of users think that our website could be enhanced and are very interested in it. The tutor and the other group members simultaneously offered helpful ideas after hearing what was said. The one that emphasizes teamwork, in my opinion, is the most satisfying because everyone has improved greatly in terms of cooperation and task division. Additionally, I've become a better leader who can bring people together and let them play to their strengths.

Final Delivery

We eventually finished the full project after a semester of development and problem-fixing. The user team page is finished in the final edition. Users can design the playground they want to use, the games they want to play, and the friends they want to hang out with. Following creation, they are saved in the database before being presented on the front end of the website. Events may be listed and shown.
